Assistive Tech
Makeathon

26-27 April, 2014 Brown University, Providence, RI

Registration is now closed. Get in touch if you have any questions!

Build for Good.

Brown University STEAM and the SpeakYourMind Foundation are partnering to bring together interdisciplinary teams of makers and hackers for a weekend long makeathon.

Together we will create innovative assistive hardware and software for individuals with visual, hearing, motor, and speech disabilities.

FAQ

What is a makeathon?

A makeathon, similar to a hackathon, is a weekend long event for building, creating, and designing products.

The Assistive Tech Makeathon focuses on both hardware and software for assistive technologies. Hacking, making, and building is encouraged!

Timing

April 26th, 10 AM - 6 PM and April 27th, 11 AM - 4PM at the Brown Design Workshop.

We’ll kick things off on Saturday at 10 AM, work until later in the evening, get a good night’s rest, and come back Sunday 11 AM. Judging begins at 2 pm Sunday, after which we’ll wrap up with a closing ceremony and prizes at 4 PM.

Travel

The Makeathon will be held on Brown University’s campus in Prince Lab Room 222, 184 Hope Street, Providence, RI. Free Parking will be available on the corner of George and Hope Street.

If you’re traveling from Boston, you can take the MBTA Providence/Stoughton Line or the Amtrak Acela Express to get to Providence. From the Providence station, Brown is a short 15 minute walk.

How do I register?

Register right here!

If you're signing up with a team, please make sure all members of your team register. We will also be helping form teams on the day of the Makeathon. Students from all universities and high schools are welcome. Members of organizations working in the field, or anyone interested, are also welcome!

What should I bring?

Anything you have to make/hack with!

We will provide Arduinos, Sparkfun Inventor kits, 3D printers (both Makerbot 2 and 2X), a laser cutter, electronic tools, soldering irons, oscilloscopes, power supplies, measuring equipment, hand tools, drill presses, horizontal and vertical bandsaws, vinyl cutters, and a bunch of other stuff.

What if I don't have much experience?

Worry not! Makeathons are great places to learn new things. We’ve put together a series of workshops for those who want more experience building, printing, processing etc. We will also have a series of guests and professionals floating around the makeathon ready to help you with ideas for assistive technologies!

What should I not bring?

Firearms, knives, and weapons of any kind are banned. Attendees of the makeathon must abide by the Code of Conduct.

Did someone say prizes?

Of course! Just one of the many prizes for the Makeathon is an Xbox and Xbox Kinect! Other fun hacking prizes will be announced at the event.

Any other questions?

Feel free to shoot us an email at makeathon@brownsteam.org!
